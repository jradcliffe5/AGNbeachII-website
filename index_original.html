AGN on the Beach II

A multi-scale view of jetted AGN

Join observers and simulators for a deep dive into how relativistic jets and compact radio outflows shape their host galaxies across cosmic scales.

Follow key dates Programme updates

About the Workshop

“AGN on the Beach II: A multi-scale view of jetted AGN” will gather the community in Diani, Kenya to connect breakthroughs in high-resolution observations, hydrodynamical simulations, and theory. The meeting builds on progress with modern radio/mm facilities to confront open questions about how jets launch, how they evolve across environments, and how they influence galaxy evolution.

  • Scope: From black hole accretion physics to galaxy-wide feedback and ISM coupling.
  • Format: Five days of invited talks, contributed presentations, focused discussions, and working sessions that bridge observers and modellers.
  • Outcome: Establish a coherent framework for future multi-wavelength campaigns and simulations targeting low-luminosity and compact jets.

Scientific Focus

Theme A

Jet Launching & Accretion

Explore how black hole spin, magnetic fields, and accretion flows trigger and sustain relativistic outflows.

Theme B

Morphology Across Scales

Compare radio morphologies from parsec to kiloparsec scales and link them to environmental drivers.

Theme C

Feedback & Galaxy Evolution

Quantify how low-luminosity and compact jets couple to the ISM, regulate star formation, and influence galaxy growth.

Theme D

Future Campaigns

Design coordinated multi-wavelength observations and simulations to test emerging theoretical frameworks.
